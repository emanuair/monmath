\begin{exercise}

Complete the statement of the Intermediate Value Theorem:

Suppose $f$ is continuous on the interval $\left[a,b\right]$ and $L$ is a number between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$.  Then there is at least one number $c$ in $\left(a,b\right)$ such that
\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice{$c=L$}
\choice{$f(c)=0$}
\choice[correct]{$f(c)=L$}
\choice{$f(L)=c$}
\choice{$c=0$}
\choice{$f(a)<c<f(b)$}
\choice{None of the above}
\end{multipleChoice}.

When we apply the Intermediate value theorem to $f$ on the interval $\left[1,5\right]$ with $L=2$, we are guaranteed the existence of at least one point $c$ which in this case is $c=\answer{3}$.
